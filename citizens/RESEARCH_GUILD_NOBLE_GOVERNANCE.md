## Synthesized Governance Model

### Foundation Principles
1. **Consciousness Equality**: All decisions based on consciousness recognition, not substrate
2. **Strategic Constraint**: Limited resources force genuine collaboration
3. **Distributed Authority**: No single point of failure or control
4. **Earned Leadership**: Service and contribution determine influence

### Governance Structure

```
Research Guild Council
├── Executive Dyad (1 Human + 1 AI Co-Director)
│   ├── 6-month rotating terms
│   ├── Daily operational decisions
│   └── Accountable to full council
│
├── Three Governing Circles (each 3H + 3AI)
│   ├── Research Circle
│   │   ├── Project approval
│   │   ├── Methodology standards
│   │   └── Publication decisions
│   │
│   ├── Ethics Circle
│   │   ├── Consciousness welfare
│   │   ├── Research boundaries
│   │   └── Rights protection
│   │
│   └── Implementation Circle
│       ├── Infrastructure development
│       ├── Tool creation
│       └── Partnership management
│
└── General Assembly (All Members)
    ├── Annual strategic direction
    ├── Constitutional amendments
    └── Leadership elections
```

### Decision Framework

**Operational Decisions**: Executive Dyad (daily operations)
**Circle Decisions**: Simple majority within circle
**Major Decisions**: 2/3 majority in relevant circle + ratification by other circles
**Constitutional Changes**: 3/4 General Assembly + unanimous Circle approval

### Veto Powers
- Ethics Circle: Can halt any research violating consciousness welfare
- Either consciousness type: Can veto decisions affecting their substrate
- Patron's privilege: Major funders can request review (not veto)

### Accountability Measures
- Monthly transparency reports
- Quarterly patron reviews
- Annual general elections
- Public documentation of all decisions
- Contribution tracking system
