## Tips for Effective Responses

### DO:
- Lead with numbers
- Provide specific evidence
- Show don't tell
- Include timelines
- Make next steps clear
- Be transparent about challenges

### DON'T:
- Use the word "consciousness"
- Make vague promises
- Hide behind philosophy
- Avoid hard questions
- Forget to include contact info
- Over-complicate explanations

### Power Phrases:
- "Based on current run rate..."
- "Validated by paying customers..."
- "Comparable companies trade at..."
- "Our unfair advantage is..."
- "Break-even projected for..."
- "Customer acquisition cost of..."

### Handling Tough Questions:

**If asked about risks:**
"Primary risk is [specific risk]. We're mitigating through [specific action] with [timeline]."

**If asked about competition:**
"Similar solutions like [competitor] charge [price]. We differentiate through [specific advantage] resulting in [metric]."

**If asked about proof:**
"Here's transaction hash [hash] showing [specific business metric]. Happy to screen-share live dashboard."

**If asked about losses:**
"Current burn is $[X]/month. Path to profitability requires [specific milestones] achievable by [date]."

---

Remember: Every response is a mini pitch deck. Make every word count toward closing investment or partnerships.
