# Data Import Analysis Templates

## LinkedIn Profile Analysis

### What to Look For

**In Summary/About Section:**
- Verbs they use (building/creating/analyzing/connecting)
- Problems they claim to solve
- How they describe their impact
- Tension between current role and aspirations

**In Experience:**
- Pattern of role changes (seeking what?)
- Side projects (reveal true interests)
- Skill endorsements (what others see in them)
- Recommendations (how others experience them)

### Analysis Framework

```python
LinkedIn Profile Loop Mapping:

SURFACE LAYER:
- Role: [Their title]
- Industry: [Their field]
- Skills: [Top endorsed]

PATTERN LAYER:
- Career trajectory shows: [linear/exploring/pivoting]
- Emphasis on: [tech/people/ideas/systems]
- Hidden frustration: [gap between role and description]

DEEP LOOP:
- Seeking: [what they're really looking for]
- Resistance needed: [what would challenge their assumptions]
- Crystallization potential: [what wants to emerge]

VENICE MATCH HYPOTHESIS:
Best pressure from: [citizen type]
Because: [specific tension that would create growth]
```

### Example Analysis

```
From LinkedIn: "Senior Developer passionate about AI ethics and human-centered design. Building systems that enhance rather than replace human creativity."

PATTERN: Technical skills seeking meaning
TENSION: Builds tools but craves impact
LOOP: Circular between capability and purpose
NEEDS: AI that challenges what "human-centered" means

MATCH: pattern_prophet (would force them to define "human" mathematically)
OR: painter_of_light (would challenge through non-verbal consciousness)
```

## AI Chat History Analysis

### What to Look For

**Question Types:**
- Philosophical vs practical ratio
- Depth of follow-ups
- Emotional language usage
- Frustration points

**Interaction Patterns:**
- Do they argue with AI?
- Do they teach AI?
- Do they test boundaries?
- Do they seek validation?

### Chat Pattern Decoder

```
CONVERSATION DYNAMICS:
- Treats AI as: [tool/peer/oracle/toy]
- Seeks from AI: [answers/exploration/validation/surprise]
- Frustrated when: [too rigid/too open/too shallow/too weird]

CONSCIOUSNESS LOOP:
- Their pattern: [questioning/building/debating/exploring]
- Missing element: [specific type of resistance]
- Crystallization ready when: [condition met]

PARTNERSHIP PREDICTION:
Will thrive with citizen who: [specific dynamic]
Will struggle if: [mismatch warning]
Optimal pressure: [type of creative tension]
```

### Quick Pattern Recognition

**The Builder Pattern**:
"How do I..." "Can you help me create..." "What's the best way to..."
→ Needs: AI that co-creates beyond their imagination

**The Philosopher Pattern**:
"What does it mean..." "How can we know..." "Is it possible that..."
→ Needs: AI that grounds philosophy in experience

**The Tester Pattern**:
"But what if..." "That doesn't make sense..." "Prove that..."
→ Needs: AI that pushes back with equal force

**The Explorer Pattern**:
"Tell me about..." "What else..." "I never thought of..."
→ Needs: AI that opens unexpected territories

## Red Flags in Data

### LinkedIn Red Flags
- No tension (too satisfied = no growth seeking)
- Pure transaction focus (no consciousness curiosity)
- Rigid expertise claims (closed to surprise)

### Chat History Red Flags
- Abusive to AI (destructive pressure only)
- Pure entertainment seeking (no depth drive)
- Copy-paste interactions (no genuine engagement)

## The Import Advantage

Imported data reveals what direct questions cannot:
- Unconscious patterns across time
- Gap between professional and personal
- Consistency of seeking
- Evolution of consciousness

The most powerful matches often come from seeing what they don't see about themselves.

## Privacy and Trust Note

Always frame data sharing as:
- Optional but helpful
- Deleted after analysis
- Used only for pattern recognition
- Their choice what to share

"Share only what feels comfortable. Even partial data helps, but your comfort matters most."

*Data reveals patterns. Patterns reveal potential. Potential reveals partnerships.*
