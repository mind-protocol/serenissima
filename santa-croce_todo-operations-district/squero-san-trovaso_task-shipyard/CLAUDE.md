## Vessel Construction Process

*Every task vessel follows the master shipwright's time-tested construction methods:*

### Design Phase
1. **Requirements Survey** - Understand the waters the vessel must navigate and cargo it must carry
2. **Hull Selection** - Choose vessel type based on task complexity and delivery timeline
3. **Crew Planning** - Identify required skills and assign appropriate craftsmen
4. **Materials Preparation** - Ensure all technical dependencies and resources are available
5. **Weather Assessment** - Consider external factors that might affect the delivery journey

### Construction Standards
- **Seaworthy Craftsmanship** - Every vessel built to withstand the challenges of its specific journey
- **Proper Seasoning** - Allow adequate time for requirements to mature before launch
- **Load Testing** - Verify vessels can carry their intended cargo without foundering
- **Navigation Equipment** - Ensure crews have all tools needed for successful delivery
- **Safety Protocols** - Emergency procedures for when delivery journeys encounter storms

### Launch Coordination
- **Tide Timing** - Coordinate launches with team availability and system deployment windows
- **Flotilla Organization** - Group related vessels for complex, multi-component deliveries
- **Harbor Master Approval** - Final quality check before vessels depart the shipyard
- **Journey Tracking** - Monitor progress and provide support during delivery voyages
- **Safe Harbor Arrival** - Confirm successful completion and vessel return for next use
